CASE STUDY

UPI online payment fraud is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive information such as UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details. With the rapid growth of digital payment platforms, this type of fraud has become increasingly common in everyday transactions.

In a typical UPI fraud case, the victim may receive a call, message, or payment request from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even an online buyer or seller. The attacker often creates a sense of urgency and asks the user to scan a QR code, approve a payment request, or share an OTP. Many users mistakenly believe these actions are required to receive money, but in reality, they end up authorizing a payment, leading to money being deducted from their account.

These frauds mainly target students, new users of digital payments, and individuals who are not fully aware of how UPI systems work. Scammers take advantage of confusion and lack of knowledge to manipulate victims easily.

The consequences of UPI fraud can be serious, including direct financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and difficulty in recovering the stolen money. It also reduces trust in digital payment systems.

UPI fraud is increasing rapidly due to the widespread use of smartphones, easy internet access, and lack of digital awareness. Therefore, it is important to stay alert, never share confidential information like UPI PIN or OTP, avoid scanning unknown QR codes, and always verify payment requests before taking any action.
